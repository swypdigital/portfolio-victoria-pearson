### 2. Poor Man's Copyright (Mail to Yourself)
**Cost:** $0.73 per envelope (USPS certified mail)
**Protection Level:** MEDIUM
**Legal Weight:** Moderate evidence of creation date

**Action Steps:**
1. **Print complete source code** for each major system (or export as PDF)
2. **Include cover page** with:
   - Your name, address, date
   - Title of work (e.g., "GenCraft Meta-Generation Architecture")
   - Copyright notice
   - Brief description
3. **Seal in envelope** and mail to yourself via **USPS Certified Mail**
4. **DO NOT OPEN** when it arrives
5. **Store safely** with postmark visible (proof of creation date)

**Why This Works:**
- USPS postmark is official government timestamp
- Sealed envelope proves you had this content on this date
- Admissible in court as evidence of prior art

**Time Required:** 3-4 hours
**Cost:** $5-10 total (certified mail for 6 projects)

### 4. Copyright Registration (Affordable)
**Cost:** $45-65 per work
**Protection Level:** HIGHEST
**Legal Weight:** Statutory damages + attorney's fees if you sue

**Action Steps:**
1. **Register with US Copyright Office** (copyright.gov)
2. **What to register:**
   - Source code as "literary work" ($65 per system)
   - Architecture diagrams as "visual works" ($45)
   - Documentation as "literary work" ($45)
3. **Recommended priority:**
   - GenCraft/Maverick Stack (highest value) - $65
   - InterviewPlus (most complete) - $65
   - TokenCalc (quickest to market) - $65
   - **Total: $195 for 3 most valuable assets**
4. **How to file:**
   - Online at copyright.gov/registration
   - Upload PDF of source code (first 25 pages + last 25 pages acceptable)
   - Receive certificate in 3-6 months

**Why This Works:**
- Only way to get statutory damages ($150K per infringement)
- Allows you to sue for attorney's fees (otherwise you pay out of pocket)
- Required before filing infringement lawsuit
- Certificate is official government recognition of ownership

**Time Required:** 2-3 hours per registration
**Cost:** $195 for top 3 assets (HIGHLY RECOMMENDED)

## üíº LICENSING/SALE PROTECTION PROTOCOLS

### Before Sharing IP with VCs or Buyers:
1. **Always require signed NDA first** (no exceptions)
2. **Share only what's necessary:**
   - Architecture diagrams (not source code)
   - Demo videos (not live code)
   - Feature descriptions (not implementation details)
3. **Watermark all documents:**
   - Add "CONFIDENTIAL - Property of Victoria Pearson" footer
   - Include date and recipient name
4. **Track who has what:**
   - Spreadsheet: Who, What shared, Date, NDA signed (Y/N)
5. **Never grant "option" or "exclusive negotiation" without compensation**
   - If VC wants exclusivity, charge $5K-10K for 30-day option

### During Licensing Negotiations:
1. **Hire attorney BEFORE signing** (use first revenue, $2K-5K for contract review)
2. **Key terms to protect:**
   - Retain ownership of core IP (GenCraft engine)
   - License only specific use rights
   - Audit rights (can verify they're not exceeding license terms)
   - Termination clause (you can revoke if they breach)
3. **Escrow for source code:**
   - Don't give source code until payment clears
   - Use escrow service (Escrow.com, $50-200 fee)

### During Sale Negotiations:
1. **Letter of Intent (LOI) first:**
   - Non-binding agreement on price, terms
   - Gives you negotiating leverage
   - Free templates available
2. **Due diligence preparation:**
   - Organize all IP documentation (GitHub commits, copyright certificates, NDAs)
   - Prove clean ownership (no co-creators, no employer claims)
3. **Retain license-back rights:**
   - If selling InterviewPlus, retain right to use core GenCraft engine
   - Never sell GenCraft itself (license only)

## üö® RED FLAGS: When You NEED an Attorney

Hire IP attorney ($300-500/hour) if:
1. **Someone copies your work** and you want to sue
2. **VC wants to invest** (term sheet review critical, $2K-5K)
3. **Licensing deal > $50K** (contract review essential, $2K-5K)
4. **Sale offer received** (M&A attorney needed, $5K-20K)
5. **Employment contract with IP assignment clause** (review before signing, $500-1K)
6. **Trademark opposition** (someone challenges your trademark, $3K-10K to defend)

**How to Find Affordable IP Attorney:**
- Law school clinics (free or low-cost)
- SCORE mentors (free business advice, can refer to pro bono attorneys)
- State bar association (often has reduced-fee referral programs)
- Defer fees until after deal closes (some attorneys work on contingency for licensing deals)
